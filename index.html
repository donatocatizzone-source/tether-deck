Frequently Asked Questions

Addressing common questions about data privacy, reliability, and our technology.

How do you get data from apps like Tinder or Uber without an API?

We use an "Over-the-Top" integration strategy. Most modern apps allow users to share content (rides, profiles, listings) via public web links. Tether simply acts as the recipient of that share action, parsing the public web page for the necessary metadata (Location, Time, Identity) without needing backend access to the source app.

Won't this trigger constant false alarms?

No. We utilize a Graded Response System. We don't call the police the moment a timer hits zero. First, we send a vibration nudge (Grade 1). Then, a local device alarm (Grade 2). Only after multiple failures to respond do we escalate to your social circle (Grade 3) or authorities (Grade 4 - Premium).

Can you actually track an Uber ride without their API?

Yes. When you share a "Trip Status" link from Uber or Lyft, it opens a public web view of your ride. Tether parses this to get your destination. Additionally, our Route Monitor feature uses your phone's own GPS to detect if you stop unexpectedly or deviate significantly from the optimal route to that destination.

Why wouldn't I just text a friend?

Friends fall asleep, forget to check their phones, or hesitate to "overreact." Tether is an automated Dead Man's Switch that never sleeps. Furthermore, Tether instantly uploads photo evidence (license plates, faces) to a secure cloud vault, ensuring it is preserved even if your phone is lost or damaged‚Äîsomething a text message cannot guarantee.
